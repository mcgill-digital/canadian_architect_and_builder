We regret to note the death on March 21, at Toronto, of the wife of <person>C. H. Acton Bond</person>, architect, of Toronto.



Mr. <person>W. L. Symons </person>and Mr. <person>Edmund Burke</person>, of Toronto, will probably attend the International Convention of Architects to be held in London in July next.



Mr. <person>Fred T. Hodgson</person>, the well known architect and technical writer of Collingwood, recently celebrated, with his wife, the 50th anniversary of their wedding. They received the congratulations of a large number of friends throughout Canada and the United States, and were presented with addresses by a number

of the fraternal organizations with which Mr. Hodgson is connected.



Mr. <person>Lawrence J. Gomme</person>, who has been for the past five and a quarter years assistant librarian, under Mr. <person>Rudolph Dircks</person>, at the <assoc>Royal Institute of British Architects</assoc>, sailed yesterday (Thursday) in the Allan liner, Sicilian, for Montreal, where lie is taking up a business. Mr. Gomme, who is a nephew of the clerk to the London County Council, takes with him the goodwill of all who have come into contact with him at 9, Conduit-street, W., and have experienced his courtesy and intimate knowledge of the books and pamphlets there shelved.
